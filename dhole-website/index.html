About dholes

The dhole (pronounced dole) also known as the whistling dog. They are classified with wolves and foxes in the canine family.

Dholes are a bit strange when it comes to fitting them in to dog subfamilies, they have shorter jaws and females can have up to 12 pups per litter.

They are fast runners and good swimmers. This helps them survive the habitat they share with tigers and other predatory animals.

Fun Facts

  • They live about 10 years in the wild up to 16 in captivity
