About dholes

The dhole (pronounced dole) also known as the whistling dog. They are classified with wolves and foxes in the canine family.

The dhole is a bit strange when it comes to fitting them in to dog subfamilies, they have shorter jaws and females can have up to 12 pups per litter.

They are fast runners and good swimmers. This helps them survive the habitat they share with tigers and other predatory animals.

Habitat and Diet

The dhole is found in eastern and southern Asia. They vary depending on their habitat, but their tail is fox-like, usually with a black tip. They also have a white belly, chest and feet.

Acording to animals.sandiegozoo.org, the dhole maintains a large territory. "They maintain a very large territoryâ€”up to 34 square miles (88 square kilometers)!".

Dholes are great at communicating and use a whistle to communicate with each other. They usually hunt in a group with one lead dog.

Dholes prey upon hoofed mammals such as deer, goats, sheep, wild pigs and buffalo, just to name a few.

Fun Facts

  • They live about 10 years in the wild, and up to 16 in captivity.
  • The dhole can whistle, scream, mew and even cluck.
